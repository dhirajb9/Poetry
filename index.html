About Me

Hello, I'm Dhiraj Bhattacharjee, a senior at Rutgers Business School. I began writing poetry to prioritize connection and human emotion. For me, poetry has developed into a means of expressing what is frequently left unsaid. If even one person has felt seen, understood, or inspired through my words, then I’ve done what I set out to do. My poems are pieces of who I represent—guarded, honest, and always reaching for something real. Feel free to reach out via the contact form if you have any questions, feedback, or just want to talk.

Poems

The Unsaid

Your eyes speak in silence a vivid storm beneath calm waters while your mouth tells stories with words that don't match the rhythm. One whispers truth, the other wears a mask, and I can't decide which to trust the calm or the voice

Rhythm is a dancer

Rhythm is a dancer, flowing deep as in a feeling where words cannot begin, Moving through the heart, touching the soul, bringing us together, as a whole. A story must be told Countless traits, fearless and bold, A language of passion in every assist With pace and power, no one can resist

For Him

He is my safety net, he lost the right to fall himself. Sacrificing his own peace for my benefit, his exhaustion shows in the lines of his face. knowing mistakes weren’t an option, He always takes the hit, so that I could have a better life. “Slowing down feels selfish,”¹ Like the world might crumble The gaze in his eyes– speaks of dreams deferred, of battles fought in rooms no one sees. And I wonder if he knows he deserves a chance to ease, to not take the hit, With me being the safety net this time. Thank you for having my back, brother. ¹ Inspired by Thomas Hobbes

When Winds Speak

Fierce winds engrave the sky with grace. A vortex of chaos, relentless in its chase. Majestic in its fury, the air and debris dance, Pulling lightning to the core — you only have one chance. Sheer wonder turns to ruin in the blink of an eye, A town erased beneath a shattered sky. After, only silence remains, Destruction has landed. The lens caught chaos — unfiltered, candid.

Screaming Silence

Are promises just words, or were they ever meant at all? That’s why I started questioning them, they taste too much like comfort, like reassurance dressed as truth. Actions— they scream what words only whisper. Not in tone, not in volume, not in pitch, but in reflection. In what lingers. In what’s missing when the moment passes. Most were never followed through. Some never even spoke. Just bandages on wounds that never closed, still warm, still bleeding— still burning for something real. And yet, I can respect the silence more than a lie. Because silence, at least, doesn’t pretend to promise. It’s a small step forward, the one it takes to finally stop pretending. It ends up dragging me two steps back, but I’ll always respect what’s done more than what’s spoken.

Wake up Call

Was it giving up, or finally waking up? We stopped knocking on a door, a door that never opened. Not from weakness, but from wisdom. We weren’t chosen, just tolerated. So we let go, not out of defeat, but because our spirit refused to starve in waiting. We moved on— not because we wanted to, but because we had to. We grew in the absence of light. Learned that persistence isn’t always the key, and that’s okay. We accepted the truth. And that was bold of us. A decision well made. A choice our future selves would be proud of We have been woken

Their Dream, My Flight

They were kids who never stopped growing, traded dreams for hardships in a foreign country, gave up language, gave up comfort, left behind family, to build futures they couldn't see. Parents who worked so hard, selfless, silent, sacrificed careers so we could chase ours. I see the kid in them still, hidden behind tired eyes and hands too full with work they loathe, missing time, but never love. If they had what I have, they’d fly ten times higher. They took the biggest risk, Now I owe them something beautiful.

Made to Mold

To become the fire, I had to burn the past, the one overcome with many emotions. I let go of laughter that rang hollow, shed layers of comfort, stitched by fear. The vision cracked when I stopped nodding yes, and in that shatter, I found a voice, not yet shaped, but meant to mold. It was left to dry, so the boy who walked could lead the man to run.

Those unheard

They told him to stay quiet that peace blooms in silence, not in fire. With no other choice, But his hands grew tired of praying politely, while violence diverges beyond the movement. They call it rage, he calls it his last chance, a shout no one heard until glass shattered. His voice was filled with desire, but you only listened when it shook the streets. A riot isn’t chaos, it’s a language, So those unheard will be heard forever

Trust

Trust sat between us, and ended with conflict silent and broad a weak glass bridge suspended over everything we built. One slip. one lapse of concentration A moment so brief, even time could forget but I never would Rain fell that day. Not just from the sky, but from everything we promised. Plans dissolved in silence, a future blurred at the edges. Your voice said sorry. But your eyes, your eyes couldn’t meet mine. And I learned then: when trust breaks, the word “sorry” is just a sound trying to reach something already lost.

Mother

You came here with empty hands, but carried the weight of a future unseen— a foreign land, unfamiliar words, and two sons you refused to let fall behind. You did the things you didn’t want to do, day after day, waking before the sun, working jobs that swallowed your joy, but never your hope. You carved a path through stone just so we could walk on memory foam You gave time you didn’t have, energy you couldn’t spare. Your body tired, your heart relentless. Even as cancer tried to fight, you fought harder proof that resilience was defined in the dictionary with your name We never went hungry, even when you had nothing left, you made something warm from scraps and love from thin air. My room, always made. My mess, always gone. I didn’t make it easy for you, But you always accepted the challenge You never forgot the small things How my day was, the snack I wanted, And the smallest detail from our conversation You remembered us even when the world forgot you. And through it all, you never asked for thanks. You only gave. I looked in the mirror And see the gift you gave me A reminder to never give up I love you, Mother.

My captain

You arrived when the ground beneath us was already cracking a club unsure of its own shadow, a fanbase holding its breath. And yet, you walked in like silence before a storm, eyes set on something higher, feet firm in foreign soil. You didn’t ask for trust. You earned it not with words, but with how you spoke with the ball how you carried weight that wasn’t yours to bear but you did it anyways Some wear the armband. You wore belief. there was a new meaning behind a ship that was sinking and carrying it to sail watching it with my own eyes In a place where glory once echoed, you echoed something louder than banners or chants: through the rough patches through no rest Even if no one claps. Even if we fall again. You played like the badge was stitched to your skin— not your shirt. And I watched, not just as a fan, but as someone learning what it means to lead without needing the light. We failed you at times. You never failed us. If the sun ever rises again over Old Trafford, it will be because of the way you kept the flame burning when everything else went dark. You didn’t just play. You stayed. you will bring glory deserving of all the best trophies And in staying A statue built in your name A legend of my beloved football club

Fragile Connections

Our generation is lonelier than ever, scrolling past souls like reels, taught to cut at the first red flag, forgetting connection was never built on perfection. Courtesy feels foreign now, Patience, a language we no longer speak. Some friendships are worth the ache, worth sitting in the quiet discomfort, worth saying I was the problem, Maybe the red flags we run from are stitched to our own hands. We treat people like posts, unfollow, unfriend, delete; gone as if hearts don’t break Companionship needs boundaries, but it also needs leniency, the kind that says stay, when every voice in your head says go.

Control

In the war of egos, The loser will likely win. To swallow pride is tasting a different kind of power. An ego kept close, like fire in a candle, can light your path, but give it time, Let it spread and it burns everything you love. Control your storms, Control your outbreaks for the one who masters inner calm stands with the truest crown

Saying No

The word no holds weight More than others on the back of their tongue, a stone they’re scared to throw. Left in the back pocket What if it makes them leave? But there's a line between kindness and prioritizing others They owe themselves the courage The pride, the power to be both gentle and unmovable, to say no without hesitation No apology needed and watch who still stays.

Father

You were one out of fourteen, Born with companionship, Fled seas to conquer comfort, Leaving everyone behind. It took me a while to see your struggle, And now I understand why you acted The way you did. You gave when you had nothing left, carved time out of a day already full, so I could live easier. And still, I didn’t always see it. I wasn’t always who you hoped I’d be. I pushed back, questioned, took more than I gave. But you stayed— Strong and unshaken. All you ever wanted was for me to do better than you. And now, with every step I take, I have one thought in mind All I need is time I love you, Father

Faith

There were days I clamped my hands, not in faith, but confusion— asking a figure above if I was even heard I was told I was always being heard Why did doubt speak louder than feeling? Temples felt far, felt like a stretch and rituals slipped through my teeth like a language I forgot how to believe in. With words I couldn’t recite I questioned where I was But then I found a sudden peace And I asked if they knew what it felt like to walk through fire without becoming hot? But time taught me this: It doesn’t always look like peace. I was never alone A hand guiding me through it all a plan, not mine, but made for me. I never doubted again

Born and Bred

You wore the badge like a second skin, not always the name they praised, but always the storm behind the silence. They doubted your feet, but never your fire. Thrown into battles not yours, positions you didn’t ask for, you gave everything anyway— blood, sweat, The part stats would never count. Twenty-two years of red wasn’t just time, it was belief, a boy becoming a part on the pitch he loved. Now in Naples, you breathe differently— not louder, but freer. You are the proof that leaving isn’t quitting— it’s becoming.

Never Enough

The applause fades faster than the silence that it leaves behind, He cross finish lines only to draw new ones— every triumph tastes like a preview of the next hunger. Success feels like a dream he outgrew the second he woke into it. But he forgets there was a version of him five years ago, knees to floor, begging for exactly this. Now he watches me pray again— not out of ingratitude, but because he taught me how to never stop becoming. Yet he was not satisfied And the question is Will he ever be?

Spring

Spring feels like a second chance— like the earth forgives itself for going cold too long. The air speaks in color before words remember how. The contrast in mellow rainy days To moderately cool evenings Even silence hums softer when nature begins to breathe. I love it most because nothing asks why it bloomed it just does, as if it always knew It was worth the wait.

Eclipse

He handed you the light before he learned how to hold it. Let your needs eclipse mine because caring, to him meant fading into silence But silence is not devotion— its erasure. Your absence taught me what presence never could: that he was never meant to shrink for someone else's comfort. Now he speaks in full volume even when no one listens. Because he was not made to be someone else's lesson.

Hidden flame

Maybe he was kind all along— just wrapped in sharp edges so no one mistook him for soft. Smiles felt like open doors and he’d seen too many storms walk in. So he has learned to frown, to speak in colder tones, to armor the warmth that still flickered beneath. Kindness doesn’t mean open hands— Sometimes it hides to survive.

Resistance

He retraced every thought like Galileo watching stars shift, their stillness never fooled him. They begged him to stay silent, said his mind spun too fast. But gravity doesn’t ask for belief. It just is. He was labeled restless, relentless when all he did was see what they weren’t ready to face.

What You Carry

You don’t talk about it much anymore, but it’s still there. Not loud, Not pronounced just tucked between moments, in the way you pause before speaking, or how you look away when a certain name floats by. Or a certain moment comes back alive You’ve learned not to be bothered to smile through it, to call it “growth” even when it still aches like memory etched in muscle. Time didn’t erase it. It just taught you how to hold it without weight Some things you don’t get over— you just learn to carry without letting them hold you.

Up And Downs

Some days lift you gently— others drag you into the ground But even the fall teaches you something that flight never could. You’ve broken in pieces that now hold the strongest parts of you. Reshaped into something new like sand crossing paths with fire only to come out clearer. Nothing is wasted not the ache, not the silence, not the nights you begged for a reason. It wasn’t chaos. It was purpose And you? You were always meant to be Shining brighter than the glass just made
